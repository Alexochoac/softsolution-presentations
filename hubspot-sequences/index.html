What is a Sequence

Your follow-up,
on autopilot

⚡
Automated
Write the emails once, set the timing, walk away. HubSpot sends them on schedule until the prospect replies or the sequence ends.
👤
Personal
Every email leaves from your real Outlook inbox, no "sent via HubSpot" footer. To the prospect it looks like you wrote it that morning.
🎯
Zero leaks
Every prospect gets the same consistent follow-up. No one falls through the cracks because you forgot to follow up.

Sequences also include manual task reminders — so on LinkedIn or WhatsApp days, HubSpot tells you exactly when and what to send.

HubSpot — Connected Inboxes

Sending on Someone's Behalf

Real
inbox — not HubSpot servers
Direct
replies go to rep's inbox
Auto
logged in HubSpot
  • HubSpot is just the scheduler. The actual email is sent through the rep's connected Outlook account, so it looks and feels like a normal email, with no "sent via HubSpot" footer.
  • To send on behalf of someone: their inbox must be connected under Settings → Email Integrations, and you need permission in HubSpot to act on their behalf.
  • Replies go straight to the rep's inbox and are automatically logged in HubSpot — the conversation lives where the prospect expects it.
  • Sequences stop automatically if the prospect replies or books a meeting. Automatic out-of-office responses do not count as replies.

The Labour Risk Sequence

The Identity Probe — Step by Step

Click any step to see the email copy and the Justin Michael reasoning behind it

Day 1
The Wedge
Email
▶
Subject: Glass inspection quality
Hi [Name], I see [Company] is growing, but I am curious how do you handle human errors on your production line? Many managers tell me it is hard to find workers with the "good eye" to catch scratches, inclusions, edge chips, or defects like distortion or white haze. Are you open to a 7-minute chat about how we automate this check?
Justin Michael — The Villain Open
We open with curiosity and mirror the prospect's reality back at them. No product, no pitch. We name the pain they already feel — the impossible task of finding a "good eye." When they recognise themselves in this, they respond.
Day 2
The Soft Connection
LinkedIn
▶
LinkedIn Connection Request
Hi [Name], saw you work at [Company] in the glass sector. Adding you to my network.
Justin Michael — The Multi-Channel Surround
Same day, different channel. A second touchpoint without any pressure. No pitch, no ask — just a network connection. This plants a familiar face before the next email lands.
Day 4
The Pattern Interrupt
Email
▶
Subject: Glass inspection quality thoughts (reply thread)
Any thoughts in this? 🔮
Justin Michael — Pattern Interrupt
One sentence. One emoji. That is it. Most salespeople write long follow-ups — so one line stops the scroll. The question is open-ended and non-threatening. It re-surfaces the original email without repeating it.
Day 7
The Visual Hammer
Email
▶
Subject: Human eye vs. LineScanner (new thread)
[Name], I thought you might find this interesting. This diagram shows how human eyes miss more defects as line speed goes up. Are you seeing this gap in your Automotive or Architectural lines?
Justin Michael — The Visual Hammer
We introduce a chart as a new thread — so it lands fresh in the inbox. The "Ghost Zone" diagram shows the growing gap between human accuracy and line speed. We let the data make the argument. We ask a binary question that segments the prospect at the same time.
Day 8
The Call — Sequence Pauses
Call
▶
Manual Task: Call contact by phone
HubSpot pauses the sequence and creates a call task for you. The next email does not send until you mark this task complete.
Justin Michael — Human Touch at the Peak
After 3 email touches and a LinkedIn connect, the prospect has seen our name several times. This is the moment to call — warm, not cold. The sequence pauses so no email fires until you have made the attempt.
Day 12
The Segment Probe
Email
▶
Subject: Rejections (new thread)
"[Name], do you have more trouble with surface defects or screen print defects (Automotive) or internal defects like fingerprints? Most of our partners use our scanners to stop these invisible costs. If it makes sense, I can send you the technical spec for your specific line."
Justin Michael — The Segment Probe
A specific binary question that shows we know their world — and tells us which product to pitch next. It feels personal because it is. The prospect has to think to answer it.
Day 14
The Breakup
LinkedIn · WhatsApp
▶
LinkedIn InMail or WhatsApp
"[Name], I haven't heard back, so I assume automated quality is not a priority right now. I will stop my emails. If you ever need to stop the $50k crane bill from an architectural reject, you have my details."
Justin Michael — The Permission Breakup
The breakup releases them with dignity and leaves a memorable anchor (the $50k crane bill). No pressure. But they will remember us.
HubSpot — Editing an Ongoing Enrollment

How to Edit a Live Sequence

Click any screenshot to zoom in

① Open the contact → scroll to the Sequences card to see the active enrollment
② Click Edit — past steps are locked, future steps can be delayed or edited
③ Use Pause to temporarily stop, or Unenroll to remove the contact entirely
When to Modify

A prospect called or you have recent information to further customize. Edit the next email step to send before it fires.

When to Pause

You receive an automatic reply from a prospect, he will be on holiday and asked you to follow up next month. You can pause or edit the date of the next action.

When to Unenroll

A customer got caught on the sequence by mistake. Remove them immediately, no more automated emails should fire.

Plan your batch sizes carefully. Enrolling too many at once means a flood of call tasks landing on the same day.
