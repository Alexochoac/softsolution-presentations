Por qué inspeccionar

Por qué la automatización de la inspección tiene sentido makes sense

⚠️ Without Inspection Automation
    Human Limitations
  • Operator fatigue — detection rates drop significantly over long shifts
  • As line speed increases, operator effectiveness declines — higher throughput means more defects slip through undetected 📈
  • 100% manual inspection is impossible at production speed
  • Distortion & anisotropy are invisible to the naked eye — no matter how skilled the operator
    • Business Impact
  • Inconsistent quality & frequent customer complaints
  • Construction site rejections — glass already delivered & installed means transport, rework & project delays 💰
  • Automotive: one defect triggers a full batch return — shipping & material costs on the entire load 💰
  • Increased liability risks & reputational damage
  • New item
✓ LineScanner & Osprey
  • 100% inspection of every glass, every shift — no fatigue, no blind spots
  • Real-time process control & quality feedback
  • Prove quality to customers — a documented defense against claims
  • Database & archive for shift accountability and CpK analysis
  • Acceptance procedures for new machinery (e.g. furnaces)
  • Increase throughput & reduce costly downstream rejects
  • Mandatory as per industry standards
  • Detects distortion, anisotropy & surface defects invisible to the human eye

La Tecnología

Cómo el LineScanner detecta lo que otros no ven sees what others miss

1Telecentric light — uniform illumination independent of glass position
2Linear image sensor — real scanning line-by-line, not a snapshot camera
3Glass — inspected continuously at full production speed
4Electronic module — 16-bit processing at extreme speed

1Low-contrast detection: Identifies defects 8-bit systems cannot see — subtle inclusions, coating variations, micro-scratches.
2Transmission homogenization: Glass from 15% to 99% processed with the same setup — identical results across all types.
3Every pixel is equal: No variation by position — always consistent, eliminating false positives and unnecessary rejections.
4Extreme processing speed: No compromise on throughput. Compatible with automotive and railway production rates.
📷 Camera-based Systems
  • Snapshot coverage: Takes periodic frames — gaps between shots mean defects between frames go undetected
  • Motion blur at speed: Fast production lines cause image blur, reducing detection accuracy
  • 8-bit depth: Only 256 grey levels — low-contrast defects are invisible
  • Position-dependent illumination: Uneven lighting across the field of view distorts results
  • Recalibration required: Sensitivity must be re-tuned for every glass type and transmission level
  • False positives & rejects: Pixel variation by position triggers unnecessary alarms

✓ LineScanner
  • 100% line-by-line scanning: Every millimetre of glass is inspected — zero gaps, zero blind spots
  • No motion blur: Linear sensor reads one line at a time, perfectly synchronised with production speed
  • 16-bit depth: 65,536 grey levels — detects the subtlest inclusions, coating variations and micro-scratches
  • Telecentric illumination: Uniform light field independent of glass position — consistent results everywhere
  • One setup for all glass: 15% to 99% transmission handled automatically with the same configuration
  • Every pixel is equal: No positional variation — eliminates false positives and unnecessary rejections
